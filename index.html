Our Ispiration

Absolutely, there are numerous inspirational figures who have made significant contributions to the agricultural field. These individuals not only made groundbreaking contributions to agricultural science and practice but also inspired generations with their dedication to sustainability, innovation, and social justice in the agricultural sector.

M. S. Swaminathan

A renowned agricultural scientist and the "Father of the Green Revolution in India," Swaminathan's work revolutionized Indian agriculture. One of his notable quotes is: "The Green Revolution has an entirely different meaning to me now. It's the recognition that food security is a part of national security."

Verghese Kurien

Known as the "Father of the White Revolution," Kurien transformed India's dairy industry. His famous quote is: "India's development lies in agriculture. India's strength lies in agriculture. The world will only listen to us if we show what we are doing in agriculture."

Norman Borlaug

Although not Indian by birth, Borlaug's contributions to India's agriculture were significant. He introduced high-yielding wheat varieties during the Green Revolution. His quote resonates with farmers worldwide: "You can't build a peaceful world on empty stomachs and human misery."

Chhaganlal K. Parekh

A pioneer in organic farming in India, Parekh emphasized sustainable agriculture. His quote reflects this ethos: "Organic farming is a return to nature. It is a harmonious blend of tradition, innovation, and science.".

Vandana Shiva

A prominent environmental activist and advocate for sustainable agriculture in India, Shiva has said, "The earth is not dead matter. She is alive. Now begin to speak to the earth as you walk. You can speak to the earth with your mind. She will respond to you."

George Washington Carver

was an American botanist and inventor. The exact day and year of his birth are unknown; he was born into slavery in Missouri, either in 1861, or January 1864. Carver was a progressive agricultural thinker, encouraging farmers to submit soil samples for analysis to determine causes of poor yield.
